Years prior, you made the decision to slam on the breaks for a crossing squirrel.
The car screeches to a halt, and the frightened squirrel escapes within a second of his life.
You continue onwards.
Unbeknowst to you, the squirrel pledges to you a life debt.
He vows to come to your aid in your time of need.
The years pass.
Times grow dark.
Soon, the entire continent is set ablaze.
Bombs drop from the sky night after night.
The situation is dire.
Every man is needed for the defense of King and Country.
The RAF knocks upon your door.
You too, will defend your home.
They shall not pass.
Bullets tear through the clouds, turning them into shreds.
You navigate your plane through the war-torn skies.
But you are outnumbered.
The enemy begins to creep forward, pressing their advantage.
You watch as all your brothers begin to fall.
Bullets riddle your plane.
You hold out with your last ounce of strength.
Then, in your darkest hour, the squirrel arrives.
Sunbeams radiate off his wings.
You watch as he dives headfirst into the action, buying you time.
You drag your limping plane to the nearest airfield.
You decide to look back.
For a glorious moment, he is tearing through the enemy, bringing down plane after plane.
He seems invincible.
But numbers soon start to take their toll.
Wave after wave of enemy fighters surge forwards like the endless tides of the Atlantic.
He is overwhelmed.
You watch as his plane bursts into flames.
His life debt has finally been paid.
You land on the airfield.
Men and women start stream out of the barracks.
"You're a hero!" They say
"No," You reply.
"I was saved by one."
